4. **Add colour to at least two meals per day.** Vegetables and fruit (especially fermented or fibre-rich ones like sauerkraut, berries, broccoli, or lentils) are the biggest single upgrade for digestion and mood. 5. **Hydrate before you're thirsty.** Low-level dehydration is one of the most common (and overlooked) causes of low energy and sluggish digestion. Aim for 2–3 litres of water daily, especially on days you exercise. Herbal teas count.

### 1. Chicken & Quinoa Power Bowl

- **Approx. calories**: ~580 kcal per serving
- **Why this suits you**: High protein for muscle support, complex carbs from quinoa for sustained energy, and plenty of veg for digestion. Fits neatly into a lunch or dinner slot within your ~3,175 kcal guide.
- **Prep time**: 30 minutes

**Ingredients:**
- 200g chicken breast
- 80g (dry weight) quinoa
- 1 tin chickpeas, drained
- 1 red pepper, sliced
- 1 courgette, diced
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp cumin, pinch of salt
- Juice of 1 lemon
- Handful of fresh parsley or rocket

**Method:**
1. Cook quinoa per packet instructions (~15 mins). Set aside.
2. Toss chicken breast in 1 tbsp olive oil, paprika, cumin, and salt. Pan-fry or grill for 6–8 minutes each side until cooked through. Rest, then slice.
3. Toss pepper, courgette, and chickpeas in the remaining olive oil and roast at 200°C for 20 minutes.
4. Assemble: quinoa base, roasted veg and chickpeas, sliced chicken on top. Drizzle with lemon juice and scatter parsley.

**Nutritional highlights:** ~45g protein, ~55g carbs, ~18g fat. Quinoa is a complete protein and excellent for muscle recovery. Chickpeas add fibre for gut health. Vitamin C from pepper enhances iron absorption.

### 2. Coconut Lentil & Spinach Curry

- **Approx. calories**: ~480 kcal per serving (with rice)
- **Why this suits you**: Lentils are exceptional for digestion, sustained energy, and plant-based protein. The gut-friendly fibre and anti-inflammatory spices (turmeric, ginger) also support mood. A great batch-cook option — makes 4 portions.
- **Prep time**: 35 minutes

**Ingredients:**
- 250g red lentils, rinsed
- 1 tin coconut milk (400ml)
- 1 tin chopped tomatoes
- 200ml vegetable stock
- 1 large onion, diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp ginger (fresh or paste)
- 1 tsp turmeric, 2 tsp curry powder
- 2 large handfuls of spinach
- 1 tbsp coconut oil
- 80g brown rice per serving

**Method:**
1. Heat coconut oil in a large pan. Sauté onion for 5 minutes until soft.
2. Add garlic, ginger, turmeric, and curry powder. Stir for 1 minute.
3. Add lentils, coconut milk, tomatoes, and stock. Stir well, bring to a boil, then simmer for 20–25 minutes until lentils are tender.
4. Stir in spinach in the final 2 minutes. Season to taste.
5. Serve with brown rice.

**Nutritional highlights:** ~22g protein per serving, ~65g carbs, ~14g fat. Red lentils are rich in prebiotics, feeding good gut bacteria. Turmeric has anti-inflammatory properties linked to improved mood and gut health.

### 3. Salmon & Sweet Potato Sheet Pan Bake

- **Approx. calories**: ~620 kcal per serving
- **Why this suits you**: Omega-3 fatty acids in salmon support muscle recovery, brain function, and mood. Sweet potato provides slow-release carbs for energy. One tray, minimal washing up.
- **Prep time**: 40 minutes (mostly hands-off oven time)

**Ingredients:**
- 2 salmon fillets (~150g each)
- 2 medium sweet potatoes, cubed
- 200g tenderstem broccoli
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp dried herbs (oregano or thyme)
- Juice of ½ lemon
- Salt and pepper

**Method:**
1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Toss sweet potato cubes in 1 tbsp olive oil, garlic powder, herbs, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking tray and roast 20 minutes.
2. Push sweet potato to one side. Add broccoli and salmon fillets. Drizzle with remaining olive oil and lemon juice.
3. Return to oven for 15–18 minutes until salmon is cooked through and broccoli is slightly crisp.
4. Serve straight from the tray.

**Nutritional highlights:** ~42g protein, ~52g carbs, ~22g fat (mostly healthy unsaturated). Omega-3s from salmon actively support muscle repair post-workout. Broccoli is packed with vitamin C and sulforaphane, a powerful compound for gut health and inflammation.

### 4. Peanut Butter Overnight Oats with Banana & Berries

- **Approx. calories**: ~520 kcal
- **Why this suits you**: An ideal breakfast for sustained morning energy — complex carbs, healthy fats, and a decent protein hit. Takes 5 minutes to prepare the night before, zero effort in the morning. Supports your digestion goal with fibre from oats and berries.
- **Prep time**: 5 minutes (overnight rest)

**Ingredients:**
- 80g rolled oats
- 250ml whole milk or oat milk
- 1 tbsp peanut butter (natural, no added sugar)
- 1 tbsp chia seeds
- 1 banana, sliced
- Large handful of mixed berries (fresh or frozen)
- 1 tsp honey (optional)
- 1 scoop protein powder (optional, adds ~25g protein)

**Method:**
1. Mix oats, milk, peanut butter, chia seeds, and honey in a jar or bowl.
2. Cover and refrigerate overnight (or minimum 4 hours).
3. In the morning, top with banana and berries. Add a splash more milk if too thick.

**Nutritional highlights:** ~18g protein (without protein powder), ~72g carbs, ~16g fat. Oats are one of the best sources of beta-glucan, a soluble fibre proven to support gut health and steady blood sugar. Chia seeds provide omega-3 and additional fibre. Berries are rich in antioxidants that support brain health and mood.
